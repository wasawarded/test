Project presentation

The aim of this project is to study the word “logic” in five different languages.

This project was carried out as part of the supervised project course in the first semester of the master's degree in Natural language processing(NLP).


Why the word Logic?


Initially, we had planned to focus solely on the study of computational models applied to linguistic data. However, as we explored the underlying underlying theoretical concepts, we realized that the word “Logic” plays a fundamental role in the formalization of linguistic structures, This discovery led us to reorient our project in order to analyze in greater detail the use and conceptualization of the word “Logic”. conceptualization of the word “Logic” in linguistic corpora, while establishing correlations with Natural language processing(NLP) methods. Language selection


We decided to study the use of the word “Logic” in English, Arabic, Chinese, French and Portuguese, because the word “logic”, although universally recognized, is tinged with the cultural and linguistic particularities of each language. This richness allows us to explore its implications in a variety of contexts, from philosophy to concrete NLP applications. Our project thus aims to demonstrate how a universal concept can be reconfigured by cultures and enrich modern linguistic approaches.


In English, the term logic is strongly associated with disciplinary fields such as mathematics, computer science and analytic philosophy. The main implication lies in its role as the basis for formal reasoning and algorithmic decision-making. In artificial intelligence, for example, logic is a mainstay of expert systems and knowledge representation.
